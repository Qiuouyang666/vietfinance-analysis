Amazon adds
$13 billion in investment in AI and cloud infrastructure in India
Amazon CEO Andy Jassy met with Indian Prime Minister Modi in New Delhi on June 25 and announced an additional $13 billion to expand AI and cloud infrastructure in India, with the investment period extending until 2030.
This additional investment means Amazon’s total planned spending on AI and cloud business in India will surpass $21 billion between 2026 and 2030, making Amazon one of the world’s biggest investors in India’s AI and cloud infrastructure sector.
The new funding will be allocated to expand AWS data center capacity in Mumbai and Hyderabad. It covers customized Trainium AI chips for startups, large corporations and government institutions, Amazon Bedrock inference engines, alongside a full suite of other AI and cloud services.
From 2010 to 2030, Amazon’s cumulative investment in India will top $88 billion, with planned total investment hitting $48 billion over the next five years. The firm also pledged to create support for 3.8 million jobs, help 15 million small businesses adopt AI technologies, and deliver AI education resources to 4 million public school students.
