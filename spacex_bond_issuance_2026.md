# SpaceX Completes First Investment-Grade Bond Issuance, Raising $25 Billion

On June 23 local time, SpaceX officially launched five tranches of senior unsecured notes with maturities of 5, 7, 10, 20, and 30 years, raising at least $25 billion in total. The proceeds will be used to repay bridge loans and for general corporate purposes. It is reported that the subscription order size for this issuance exceeds $85 billion. Last week, credit rating agencies assigned an investment-grade rating to SpaceX, laying the foundation for the issuance. SpaceX's stock price also rebounded after a recent correction in tech stocks.

This bond issuance comes less than two weeks after SpaceX's listing on the Nasdaq. Part of the funds raised will support its AI expansion plans in data centers, computing hardware, power infrastructure, and other sectors, which are expected to involve tens of billions of dollars in investment.
