Morgan Stanley Issues Warning! Two Key Factors May Trigger US Stock Market Plunge, Fed Unlikely to Bail Out the Market
As US stocks stabilize and rebound, investors are seeking clues to judge whether the AI-driven rally can continue. The upcoming earnings report of Micron Technology will serve as a crucial indicator to gauge chip demand prosperity and verify the sustainability of industrial growth momentum.
Morgan Stanley points out that against the backdrop of Fed balance sheet reduction leading to liquidity tightening and a peaking upward revision pace of corporate earnings growth, the stock market is poised for sharp volatility.
Morgan Stanley: US Stocks Face Severe Tests
Led by star strategist Mike Wilson, the Morgan Stanley research team believes US equities are heading for major challenges, and the Federal Reserve is unlikely to step in to prop up the market.
Market news regarding shipping access restrictions in the Strait of Hormuz has been circulating recently. For capital markets, the core consensus is that the United States and Iran remain in dialogue, and oil tankers are gradually resuming navigation in the restricted waterway. Beyond geopolitical tensions in the Middle East, another pivotal unsettled market variable lies in whether the new Fed leadership led by Kevin Walsh will adopt a full hawkish stance or merely use hawkish rhetoric.
In a recent press conference, Walsh criticized his colleagues for failing to hit the 2% inflation target over the past five years, sending a clear signal that the Federal Reserve is shifting to a tougher policy stance firmly anchored in inflation control.
Wilson stated that Walsh’s appointment helps restore the Fed’s market credibility, which is essential for implementing debt resolution plans driven by economic growth. Since Walsh’s nomination, the ratio between the S&P 500 Index and gold prices has surged by 40%. Simply put, prioritizing policy credibility makes Walsh’s appointment a rational choice, which has long been reflected in precious metal price trends over the past decade.
Morgan Stanley emphasized that the toughest market phase has arrived. Though Walsh has not elaborated on subsequent balance sheet adjustments, balance sheet management is among the core research topics of his newly established working groups. Meanwhile, the growth pace of the Fed’s total balance sheet has already slowed down notably.
The Federal Reserve has cut monthly reserve balance sheet reduction from 40 billion US dollars to 10 billion US dollars, while the Treasury buyback scale has been halved, coupled with rising credit expansion.
All signs indicate that market liquidity is officially entering a tightening cycle. The trend will hardly reverse unless panic breaks out in monetary financing markets, US Treasury bond volatility spikes sharply or liquidity crises emerge in credit markets. In short, the biggest short-term risk for US stocks stems from liquidity contraction rather than Fed rate hikes to curb inflation.
Apart from shrinking liquidity, institutional upward revisions to corporate earnings forecasts have also peaked recently. Morgan Stanley warns that global markets will soon test Walsh’s policy commitment of accepting short-term economic pains for long-term stability.
Will Micron Become the Trigger?
Late June is traditionally a quiet earnings season with most major enterprises having released quarterly reports months in advance. Yet this year’s Wall Street market ushers in remarkable highlights. Micron Technology has emerged as a core market favorite over the past year, and its rising revenue and profits have greatly lifted its weight in influencing S&P 500 trends.
Driven by robust AI computing demand, memory chip prices have soared, fueling explosive profit growth at Micron. FactSet data shows analysts expect Micron’s adjusted earnings per share in the latest fiscal quarter to hit 20.57 US dollars, marking a nearly tenfold year-on-year surge.
Industry insiders note that Micron’s phenomenal growth mainly benefits from product price hikes instead of extra operational costs, turning new business orders directly into net profits.
The latest earnings release carries far-reaching significance. As sector valuations stay at elevated levels, investors worry that the current rally has overdrawn future growth potential. If Micron’s earnings data reveals strong underlying demand and sustained high AI-related capital expenditure, market bullish sentiment will be boosted to sustain the upward trend.
Industry insiders hold that AI remains the core mainline driving global markets. Orders and backlogs of semiconductor firms prove that chip demand far outpaces current production capacity, and leading tech giants show no signs of slowing down AI investment, with annual spending projected to jump from 400 billion US dollars in 2025 to over 700 billion US dollars.
According to FactSet statistics, NVIDIA and Micron are the two major engines driving overall earnings growth of the S&P 500. Excluding these two giants, the index’s overall expected earnings growth will slump sharply from 22% to 14.9%.
Micron has also become a core weighted stock in the Philadelphia Semiconductor Index. Institutions forecast Micron’s full-year net profit will reach 136.7 billion US dollars in 2027, close to Apple’s expected 142 billion US dollars and far exceeding Amazon and Meta’s profit forecasts.
Market insiders believe Micron is in the golden expansion cycle of the memory chip industry, and its driving force on US stock earnings will reach a new peak.
Nevertheless, market consensus suggests that the latest fiscal quarter may mark the peak of Micron’s year-on-year earnings growth rate. Analysts predict its growth pace will cool down in the next fiscal quarters, even as absolute earnings per share continue to climb.
