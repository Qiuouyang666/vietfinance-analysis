Iranian delegation suspends Iran-US negotiations to protest Trump's threatening remarks
On the 21st, the Iranian delegation, which was conducting talks between Iran and the U.S. in Bergen Hill, Switzerland, suddenly suspended talks in protest of U.S. President Trump's threatening remarks against Iran that day.

On that day, Trump warned Iran on social media to immediately stop its "proxy" actions in Lebanon, or else the U.S. would strike Iran again, "and more fiercely."

According to Iranian media, the Iranian delegation protested Trump's remarks to the U.S. side, left the venue, suspended 80 minutes of negotiations, and shifted to internal consultations. The report stated that Iran is formulating an appropriate response plan.

Meanwhile, the head of the Iranian delegation and Speaker of the Islamic Parliament, Khalibaf, posted on social media, "They would better be cautious with their words and actions; our armed forces are ready to respond to them in different ways."

In response to this incident, Lebanon's "Square" TV station reported that Iran's response was clear: unless Trump apologizes for his threatening remarks and Israeli troops withdraw from southern Lebanon, the Iranian delegation will not return to the negotiating table.

Xinhua reporters observed on site that the negotiations were extended beyond the original schedule, and it remains unknown whether the Iranian delegation will return to the negotiating table afterward.
